TGME49_255260 - Extremely High Expression Across Tachyzoites and Bradyzoites with Dramatic Silencing During Sexual Development

This gene exhibits exceptionally high expression in Toxoplasma gondii asexual stages, consistently ranking in the 98th-99th percentile across multiple experiments. Key expression patterns include:
  • Peak expression in bradyzoites (2,713 TPM, 99.66th percentile) and tachyzoites (1,593-4,767 TPM)
  • Dramatic >400-fold upregulation during oocyst sporulation
  • Complete silencing (<3 TPM) in all feline enteroepithelial sexual stages
  • Biphasic expression pattern during tachyzoite time courses
The consistent high expression in replicating stages and complete repression during sexual development suggests this gene encodes a critical component of the asexual replication machinery, possibly involved in DNA replication, cell division, or metabolic processes essential for rapid parasite multiplication but incompatible with sexual differentiation.
