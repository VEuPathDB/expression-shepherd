NCU06971 - Cellulose-responsive gene with developmental regulation and transcription factor dependencies

This gene exhibits strong upregulation in response to cellulose and complex plant biomass substrates, with expression levels reaching 144-356 TPM compared to baseline levels of 3-50 TPM on simple sugars or under starvation conditions.
  • Expression is dramatically dependent on transcription factors XLR-1, CLR-1, and CLR-2, showing 18-fold reduction in xlr-1 knockouts
  • Shows clear developmental regulation during sexual development in Neurospora crassa, peaking at mid-stage perithecia formation
  • Exhibits substrate specificity, with highest response to mixed-linkage glucan and crystalline cellulose
  • Strongly repressed by sucrose, indicating carbon catabolite repression
The consistent cellulose-responsive expression pattern and transcription factor dependencies strongly suggest this gene encodes a cellulase or cellulose-degrading enzyme critical for plant biomass utilization.
