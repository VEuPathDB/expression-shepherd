PF3D7_1408200 - Strong cyclical expression peaking in early blood stages with dramatic mRNA stabilization during development

This gene exhibits highly cyclical expression during the Plasmodium falciparum intraerythrocytic development cycle, with peak transcript abundance during ring and early trophozoite stages (8-20 hours post-invasion) followed by substantial decline in later stages. Expression shows remarkable 10-50 fold dynamic ranges across multiple independent studies, with consistently high percentile rankings (>90th percentile) during peak periods. Notably, the gene displays dramatic mRNA stabilization during development, with half-life increasing over 30-fold from ring (1.67 hours) to late schizont stage (51.12 hours). Additional stage-specific patterns include male-biased expression in gametocytes and higher expression in mosquito-derived versus laboratory-produced sporozoites, suggesting roles in early parasite establishment and sexual development.
