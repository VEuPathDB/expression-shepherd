PF3D7_1133400 - Stage-Specific Expression Pattern with Extreme Sporozoite and Late Schizont Upregulation

This gene exhibits one of the most dramatic stage-specific expression patterns in Plasmodium falciparum, with exceptionally high expression in sporozoites (>3000 TPM, 99th percentile) and late schizont stages (>1500 TPM, 96-99th percentile), while maintaining minimal expression during trophozoite stages (<50 TPM). The gene shows
  • Over 300-fold upregulation from trophozoites to schizonts during asexual development
  • Dramatically higher expression in mosquito-derived versus cultured sporozoites
  • Consistent biphasic expression patterns during intraerythrocytic development cycles
. The extreme sporozoite-specific expression and late schizont upregulation suggest this gene plays critical roles in parasite transmission, host cell invasion, or late-stage replication processes, potentially functioning in sporozoite infectivity or merozoite formation.
