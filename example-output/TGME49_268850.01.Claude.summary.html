TGME49_268850 - Dramatic upregulation in feline sexual stages and tachyzoites with cell cycle regulation

This gene exhibits extreme stage-specific expression in Toxoplasma gondii with highest levels during sexual development in the definitive feline host.
  • Shows 25-75 fold higher expression in feline enteroepithelial stages (EES1-5) compared to tissue cysts
  • Maintains high expression in tachyzoites (~1,100-2,500 TPM) but is dramatically reduced in bradyzoites
  • Displays cell cycle-dependent oscillation with biphasic peaks every 6 hours
  • Increases significantly during oocyst sporulation from day 0 to day 10
The gene appears constitutively expressed at high levels in actively replicating stages but is downregulated during chronic infection phases. This expression pattern suggests a critical role in parasite replication and sexual reproduction, potentially functioning in cell division machinery or metabolic processes required for rapid proliferation.
