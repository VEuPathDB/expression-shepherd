PBANKA_0412900 - Upregulation of expression during specific developmental stages in Plasmodium berghei

Summary of expression data for this gene:
  • This gene is significantly upregulated during the ookinete stage of Plasmodium berghei development compared to other stages, indicating stage specificity of its function.
  • It shows higher expression in female than male gametocytes and exhibits low expression in the asexual erythrocytic stages.
  • During liver-stage development, its expression increases moderately in the late stages.
  • Moreover, under stress or genetic manipulation conditions, such as dietary restriction or in the presence of sulfadiazine, as well as genetic modifications of key transcription factors, the gene exhibits distinct expression modulation, highlighting its potential role in response to environmental and genetic factors.
  • These findings collectively suggest its importance in stage-specific functions and adaptive responses in the parasite lifecycle.
