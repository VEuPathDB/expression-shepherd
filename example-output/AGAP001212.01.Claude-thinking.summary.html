AGAP001212 - Digestive system-enriched expression with strong circadian rhythmicity in Anopheles gambiae

This gene exhibits highly tissue-specific expression in Anopheles gambiae digestive tissues, with strongest expression in midgut, salivary glands, and gastric caeca across multiple developmental stages. The gene displays robust circadian rhythmicity with peak expression during subjective night and shows temporal responsiveness to blood feeding with maximum expression at 24 hours post-meal. Sexual dimorphism is evident, with generally higher expression in females, particularly in whole-body samples. The gene also responds to parasite infections and shows upregulation in insecticide-resistant mosquitoes. Given its consistent enrichment in secretory and digestive tissues, this gene likely functions in digestive or secretory processes critical for mosquito physiology and potentially vector competence.
