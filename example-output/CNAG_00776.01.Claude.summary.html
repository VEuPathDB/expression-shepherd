CNAG_00776 - Highly expressed gene shows dramatic host environment suppression and temperature-dependent regulation

This gene exhibits consistently high expression levels (typically >95th percentile) across diverse laboratory conditions in Cryptococcus neoformans, but undergoes dramatic downregulation (10-150 fold) when exposed to host environments, particularly cerebrospinal fluid. Key expression patterns include:
  • Strong upregulation during stationary phase versus exponential growth
  • Temperature-dependent responses with variable effects at 37°C
  • Significant suppression in clinical isolates from cerebrospinal fluid
  • Enhanced expression in various transcriptional regulator mutants (nrg1Δ, ada2Δ, ccr4Δ)
The gene's high constitutive expression in laboratory conditions combined with its dramatic suppression in host environments suggests it may encode a metabolic enzyme or stress response protein that is actively repressed during pathogenesis to facilitate host adaptation.
