cgd7_2940 - Stage-specific upregulation during intracellular development and natural intestinal infection

This gene shows dramatic stage-specific expression patterns in Cryptosporidium parvum, with particularly high levels during intracellular development and natural intestinal infection.
  • Expression peaks at 6-24 hours post-infection (251-323 TPM) compared to sporozoites (3.7-7.3 TPM)
  • Natural calf intestinal infection shows much higher expression than in vitro cell culture
  • Intracellular stages express 44-fold higher levels than oocysts
  • Strong antisense expression (up to 159 TPM) suggests potential regulatory function
The consistent upregulation during early-to-mid infection stages and dramatically higher expression in natural versus artificial infection conditions suggest this gene may be critical for establishing infection in the intestinal environment and responding to host-specific factors.
