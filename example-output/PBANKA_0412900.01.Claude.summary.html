PBANKA_0412900 - Ookinete-specific expression with female gametocyte bias in Plasmodium berghei

This gene exhibits dramatic ookinete-specific expression in Plasmodium berghei, reaching >2900 TPM in ookinetes compared to minimal expression in asexual blood stages.
  • Shows >100-fold upregulation in ookinetes versus other life cycle stages
  • Demonstrates female gametocyte bias with 6-fold higher expression than male gametocytes
  • Expression is regulated by AP2-O2 and AP2-O3 transcription factors
  • Exhibits early upregulation during gametocyte development with peak expression at 0 and 12 hours post-induction
The gene's temporal expression pattern suggests critical roles in mosquito-stage development, potentially involved in ookinete motility, invasion, or survival functions, with additional importance in female gametocyte biology and sexual differentiation processes.
