PBANKA_0412900 - Ookinete-specific expression with female gametocyte preference in Plasmodium berghei

This gene exhibits dramatic stage-specific expression in Plasmodium berghei, with its most striking feature being >100-fold upregulation in ookinetes (>2000 TPM) compared to all other life cycle stages. Expression patterns show sexual stage preference, with moderate levels in gametocytes (~30-60 TPM) and particularly strong female gametocyte bias (6-fold higher than males). The gene shows minimal expression in asexual blood stages (~1 TPM) and variable, generally low expression during liver stages.

  • Female gametocyte preference indicates involvement in sexual differentiation processes
  • Regulation by AP2 transcription factors suggests integration into sexual development pathways
