TGME49_268860 - Bradyzoite-specific expression with dramatic upregulation during chronic infection

This gene exhibits remarkable stage-specific expression in Toxoplasma gondii, being dramatically upregulated in bradyzoites compared to all other life cycle stages. Expression increases over 3000-fold during tachyzoite-to-bradyzoite conversion and reaches extremely high levels (>6000 TPM) in tissue cysts during chronic infection. The gene shows minimal expression in tachyzoites, merozoites, oocysts, and enteroepithelial stages, but ranks in the 99th percentile during bradyzoite differentiation across multiple experimental systems. This consistent and dramatic bradyzoite-specific expression pattern suggests the gene likely plays a critical role in chronic infection biology, potentially involved in cyst formation, maintenance, or bradyzoite-specific metabolic processes essential for parasite persistence in the host.
