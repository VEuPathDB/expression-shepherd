AGAP006348 - Salivary Gland-Enriched Expression with Circadian Regulation and Developmental Upregulation

This gene exhibits highly tissue-specific expression in Anopheles gambiae, with over 50-fold enrichment in salivary glands compared to midgut and substantial expression in chemosensory organs (maxillary palps >> antennae) and immune cells (hemocytes). The gene shows clear circadian rhythmicity with peak expression at subjective midnight and developmental upregulation during late larval stages and embryogenesis. Expression responds dynamically to
  • environmental stresses (heat shock, ivermectin treatment)
  • pathogen infections (differential patterns with Plasmodium and Wolbachia)
  • reproductive processes (blood feeding, hormonal stimulation)
. The pronounced salivary gland enrichment combined with stress responsiveness suggests potential roles in vector biology, possibly related to blood feeding, pathogen transmission, or host-seeking behaviors.
