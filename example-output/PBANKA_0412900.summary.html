PBANKA_0412900 - Exploring Gene Dynamics in Plasmodium berghei's Life Cycle: From Gametocytes to Liver Stages

This gene in Plasmodium berghei displays diverse expression patterns across various life cycle stages, highlighting its multifaceted role in parasite development and transmission. Notably, expression peaks significantly during the ookinete stage, indicating a key function in this developmental phase. Furthermore, higher expression in female gametocytes and under rapamycin induction emphasizes its involvement in sexual stage development. While expression patterns vary in liver and sporozoite stages, indicative trends suggest importance in both early and late liver maturation. Interestingly, a dietary restriction severely reduces its expression, hinting at potential nutritional regulation.
