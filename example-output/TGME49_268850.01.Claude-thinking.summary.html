TGME49_268850 - Dramatically elevated expression during sexual development with sustained high levels in tachyzoites

This gene exhibits dramatically elevated expression during sexual development in Toxoplasma gondii, with 20-25 fold increases in enteroepithelial stages (EES1-5) compared to asexual stages, reaching 2,500-2,800 TPM in the feline intestinal environment. Expression remains consistently high in tachyzoites (600-1,100 TPM) across multiple strains and time points, while being substantially lower in bradyzoites and oocyst stages. The gene shows cell cycle-dependent regulation with periodic expression patterns and varies by host cell type and infection context.
  • Peak expression occurs during sexual reproduction in feline hosts
  • Maintains high levels during rapid tachyzoite replication
  • Reduced during chronic infection and cyst formation
This expression pattern suggests critical roles in both sexual reproduction and acute infection processes.
