Tb927.3.3270 - Bloodstream form-specific expression with dramatic stage-dependent regulation

This gene shows dramatically higher expression in bloodstream forms compared to procyclic forms of Trypanosoma brucei, with expression levels consistently 5-15 fold higher in mammalian host stages. The gene exhibits peak expression in long slender bloodstream forms and shows rapid downregulation during differentiation to procyclic forms. Expression is enhanced in specialized tissues including adipose tissue and cerebrospinal fluid compared to blood. Ribosome profiling data indicates active translation in bloodstream forms, with translation efficiency markedly higher than transcription levels. The consistent bloodstream-specific upregulation across multiple experimental conditions suggests this gene plays a critical role in mammalian host adaptation, potentially involved in metabolic processes or virulence factors required for survival in the mammalian bloodstream environment.
